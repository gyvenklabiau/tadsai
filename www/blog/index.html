Time

When looking at 24 hour clock a day can be easily divided into 2, 4 and 8 segments.

When it consists of 2 each of them is 12 hours.

It can also be 4 segments of 6 hours each.

Or 8 periods of 3 hours each.

Surely the simplest and most common amount of time is one hour.

A funny way to call a 24 hour day as often the part of that time is night.

But what is the english word for 24 hour cycle?

Some like to call the 2 periods AM/PM and leave the writing of time like 1420 for military purposes.

How many minutes per day?

24 * 60 = 1440

I decided to spend 23 minutes a day writing this blog.

I might try doing it daily. I might skip a few days. And sometimes I might do some more.

However, if one thing I have learned in my 34 years of life is that time can be running faster and slower.

In some way you are in control.

But its not an easy decision.

Understanding time is something that interested me lately.

I've done experimented a bit with it.

Now it seems like a good time to start a blog.

Some might say that its a bit late for that. But as someone who never had a one I don't really care.

One thing that comes surprising me again and again is how the time is not as linear as we tend to think of it.

So perhaps its never too late for anything?
